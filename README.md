# Exploring the Effect of Information Sampling on the Relationship Between PIU and Decision Confidence
This repository contains the statistical analysis and research findings for a study investigating how Prospective Intolerance of Uncertainty (PIU) influences Decision Confidence, and the mediating role of Information Sampling in this relationship. It includes also the coding part for the descriptive analysis of the data, as well as the modelw that were used. Using a dataset of 214 participants who engaged in a sequential information-sampling "game," the study employs Multilevel Structural Equation Modeling (MSEM) and Mediation Analysis to determine if seeking more information explains why individuals with high PIU report different confidence levels. The results indicate a partial mediation, suggesting that while information sampling is a significant factor, PIU also exerts a strong direct effect on confidence.

# Key Variables of the project
- Prospective Intolerance of Uncertainty (PIU): A cognitive tendency to reduce uncertainty about future events.
- Information Sampling: The number of "tiles" participants chose to reveal before making a decision in the experimental task.
- Decision Confidence: Self-reported certainty (0–100) in the accuracy of a choice.
- Control Variables: Age, Gender, Correct Choice, Trial Number, and Response Time.

# Experimental Methodology
Participants completed 60 trials of a "game" where they flipped gray squares in a 5x5 grid to determine the majority color.
